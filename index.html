Birthday Data, 2014

A basic and quick compilation of all of the birthday messages that I recieved this year. All of the data has been pulled from private and public sources, such as facebook wall posts, twitter mentions, and texts. Data was standardized across timezones, and then these visualizations were created. Private data has been omitted for privacy.

Messages Over Time

The first visualization that we'll look as is just the messages I recieved throughout the day. The messages here are grouped by the hour, and the shaded sections represent night time. As you can see, there are less messages recieved during night, and several messages were sent early in the morning. The likely cause of this is that people woke up, remembered it was my birthday (or checked facebook), and sent me a message.

In addition to this, many messages were sent in the first hour of my birthday. This is expected, as many caring people wish happy birthday the exact second that it is 12:00pm.

Ages

Looking at the ages of the senders, we see no surprises. The largest age group is 0-20, which is the majority age of my friends and acquaintances. The largest contributing factor here is really my age, as I myself just turned 20, and have friends of similar ages. For ages above 30, most of the senders were either co-workers or family.
